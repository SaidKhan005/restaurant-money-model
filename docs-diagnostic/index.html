How It Works

The Restaurant Money Model defines a non-negotiable sequence. Every restaurant follows the same causal chain - upstream constraints must be resolved before downstream optimization has any effect.

1
DEMAND
Do I have enough guests to fill my restaurant's capacity?
2
CAPACITY
Can we serve this demand without degrading the guest experience?
3
EXPERIENCE
Did we turn the guest's time and attention into value worth repeating?
4
BEHAVIOR
Are guests being guided to higher-value decisions?
5
ECONOMICS
Which items produce the most profit per constraint?
6
PROFIT
Are we building a business that compounds, or just surviving the week?

The diagnostic scores your answers against all six layers, then identifies your primary constraint following the model's sequence rule: upstream constraints are always diagnosed first.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Restaurant Money Model?

A six-layer system that maps how restaurants actually make money. The layers - Demand, Capacity, Experience, Behavior, Economics, and Profit - follow a non-negotiable causal sequence. Each layer feeds the next. Upstream constraints must be resolved before downstream optimization has any effect.

How does the diagnostic work?

Ten questions probe signals from across all six layers. Your answers accumulate scores for each layer. The diagnostic then identifies your primary constraint following the model's sequence rule - if an upstream layer is constrained, it is diagnosed first, because fixing downstream issues won't help until the upstream constraint is resolved.

What should I do with my results?

Start with the identified constraint. The results page provides the model's specific frameworks and steps for that layer. If you see a cross-layer note, it means your constraint is affecting downstream layers - which is expected, because that is how the sequence works. Fix the constraint, and the downstream symptoms often resolve on their own.

Why doesn't this focus on food cost percentage?

The Restaurant Money Model defines economics as prime cost engineering - not food cost percentage. Food cost percentage only measures ingredient cost against revenue. Prime cost includes the labor required to produce each item, which changes the math entirely. A low food-cost item that takes 20 minutes of skilled labor may be a bleeder, not a hero.
